2D Liquid Crystals

The repository "Q-tensor 2D" is an implementation of the Landau-de Gennes (LdG) Q-tensor model for liquid crystals (LCs) based on Andrew Hicks’ "Q-tensor 3D". Our goal was to learn the LdG method for solving the energy of a system of LCs at equilibrium. Yuzhi Liu, Steven Spasov, and I built this repository in May - July 2025 under the direction of Dr. Andrew Hicks. We were funded by Carnegie Mellon University as a part of the Summer of Undergraduate Applied Mathematics Institute.

3D Liquid Crystals

There is plenty of research investigating properties of liquid crystal (LC) spheres, but little work published about LC shells, the result of removing the volume of a smaller sphere from a larger one. Yuzhi Liu, Dr. Andrew Hicks, and I are working to determine how and why different kinds of defects form in these shells. Yuzhi Liu, Steven Spasov, and I began this work in May - July 2025 under the direction of Dr. Andrew Hicks. We were funded by Carnegie Mellon University as a part of the Summer of Undergraduate Applied Mathematics Institute.

Galois Theory Research

This research uses Artin-Schreier presentations to classify Galois extensions of degree $p^6$ and $p^5$ with maximal nilpotency class over a field of characteristic $p$, for some prime $p$. From May 2023-24, I was funded by the NASA Nebraska Space Grant. My profile is featured on the NASA Nebraska Space Grant website here. On May 31st, 2024, I presented at the annual Hopf Algebras and Galois Module Theory Conference in Omaha, Nebraska. The official website can be found here. Math Education Research

Since September, 2024, I have worked under the supervision of Dr. Keith Gallagher to examine the treatment of logarithms in College Algebra-level resources. This work has resulted in one peer-reviewed conference proceeding from SIGMAA on RUME 2025. I gave a talk on this paper at the Nebraska-SE South Dakota section meeting of the MAA. Currently, I am working on a literature review to further investigate this problem.
